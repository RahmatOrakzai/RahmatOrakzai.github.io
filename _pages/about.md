# Healthcare AI, computational imaging, and digital health

I am a Lecturer in Computer Science in the [School of Computer Science and Electronic Engineering at the University of Essex](https://www.essex.ac.uk/people/ULLAH03304/Rahmat-Ullah) and a Fellow of the Higher Education Academy (FHEA).

My research develops artificial intelligence, biomedical signal-processing, and computational-imaging methods for healthcare. A central strand of my work investigates non-invasive monitoring of neurodegenerative diseases, particularly Alzheimer’s disease, using microwave and radar sensing. I also work on digital health, human activity recognition, and intelligent technologies for independent and assisted living.

## Current work

My current work includes AI-driven biomedical monitoring, intelligent sensing for personalised healthcare, and unobtrusive activity recognition for elderly care. I welcome enquiries from prospective students and collaborators working in biomedical signal processing, microwave imaging, health data, digital health, or machine learning.

## Background

I completed a PhD in Engineering at the University of Edinburgh, where I developed data-driven microwave imaging and signal-processing methods for monitoring neurodegenerative diseases in collaboration with the Centre for Clinical Brain Sciences. Before joining Essex, I was a Research and Development Associate at the University of South Wales and a Teaching Assistant at the University of Edinburgh.

I hold an MSc in Software Engineering from COMSATS University Islamabad and a BS in Information Technology from the University of Malakand.
